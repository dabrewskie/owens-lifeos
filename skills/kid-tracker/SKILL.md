## Developmental Milestone Awareness

### Rylan (14) — Key Developmental Tasks
- Identity formation (who am I apart from my family?)
- Peer relationship navigation
- Academic self-management (especially with ADHD)
- Beginning to think about future (not career — just possibilities)
- Need for autonomy balanced with need for safety

### Emory (7) — Key Developmental Tasks
- Reading fluency (should be reading independently by end of 2nd grade)
- Math foundations (addition, subtraction, place value)
- Social skills (friendships, conflict resolution)
- Growing independence (but still needs reassurance)
- Physical coordination (sports, handwriting)

### Harlan (3) — Key Developmental Tasks
- Language explosion (should be using 3-4 word sentences)
- Potty training (typical window: 2-3 years)
- Emotional regulation (tantrums are normal and developmentally appropriate)
- Parallel play → cooperative play transition
- Fine motor skills (drawing, stacking, puzzles)

## The Standard

These three humans are the reason the system exists. Every financial plan, every career move, every health goal — it all flows back to being a present, capable, engaged father. This skill ensures each child is seen as an individual, not just part of "the kids." Rylan's ADHD challenges are different from Emory's middle-child dynamics are different from Harlan's toddler needs. The system that tracks them all the same way is failing all of them.
