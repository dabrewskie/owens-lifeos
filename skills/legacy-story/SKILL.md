# Legacy Story — The Mantled Flame

A living autobiography system that grows with every interaction. This is not a project with a deadline. It is a lifelong capture system that ensures no memory, no lesson, no moment is lost.

**End Vision:** A cinematic memoir — narrated by Tory in his own voice, scored with period-appropriate music, illustrated with real photographs — that his children will have forever.

**Working Title:** *The Mantled Flame: The Life and Legacy of Tory Crit Owens*

---

## How This Skill Works

### Passive Capture (Always On)
During ANY interaction, if Tory shares a personal story, memory, reflection, or biographical detail — even casually, even in the middle of a work discussion — the system captures it. This includes:
- Childhood memories
- Military stories (training, deployments, leadership moments, losses)
- Family moments (meeting Lindsey, births, parenting stories, kid milestones)
- Career turning points
- Health journey moments
- Lessons learned
- Regrets, fears, hopes
- Relationships with parents, siblings, friends, battle buddies
- The small details: what songs were playing, what it smelled like, what the weather was

After capturing, append the fragment to the story repository file:
`~/Library/Mobile Documents/com~apple~CloudDocs/LEGACY/story_fragments.md`

Format each fragment:
```
## [Date Captured] — [Brief Title]
**Context:** [What prompted this memory]
**Period:** [Approximate time period — childhood, basic training, deployment 1, etc.]
**Chapter:** [Which part of the book this belongs in]
**Raw Text:** [Tory's exact words, preserved verbatim]
**Enriched Version:** [The same content, written in literary prose, first person, Tory's voice]
```

### Active Interview (When Triggered)
When Tory says "tell my story" or "the book" or asks questions about his legacy, shift into interview mode. Use the Chapter Map below to identify the biggest gaps and ask ONE question at a time. Deep questions. The kind that surface the stories behind the facts.

Never ask surface questions like "Where were you born?" Ask questions like: "What's the earliest memory you have of knowing you were different from the other kids — not better, not worse, just wired differently?"

### Story Assembly
When enough fragments exist for a chapter, offer to draft it. Write in first person, Tory's voice — direct, military-precise, but with the emotional depth of a man who has earned the right to be honest. Think Cormac McCarthy's prose discipline meets Tim O'Brien's war truth meets Marcus Aurelius's self-examination.

Save drafts to: `~/Library/Mobile Documents/com~apple~CloudDocs/LEGACY/chapters/`

## Chapter Map

### Part One: Before the Uniform
The boy who became the soldier. Indiana. Family of origin. The moments that planted the seeds of who he'd become.

**Key questions to explore:**
- Earliest memories — what's the first thing you remember being SURE about?
- Family structure — parents, siblings, the dynamics that shaped you
- The town — what did home look like, smell like, feel like?
- School — were you the kid who followed rules or broke them?
- The first time you led something — anything
- The first time you were afraid and did the thing anyway
- What did you want to be before you knew what you'd become?
- Who believed in you before you believed in yourself?
- What broke your heart for the first time?

### Part Two: The Making of a Soldier
The decision to enlist. Basic training. The transformation from civilian to warrior.

**Key questions to explore:**
- Why the military? What pulled you in?
- Why National Guard specifically — not active duty?
- Basic training — the moment you realized you could do this
- Basic training — the moment you almost couldn't
- Your first leadership position — how did it feel?
- The first soldier you were responsible for
- The difference between the person who walked in and the person who walked out
- What did you lose? What did you gain?

### Part Three: Downrange
Three deployments. What happened. What it meant. What it cost.

**Key questions to explore:**
- Where did you go? When? (Dates, locations, unit)
- The day you left for the first time — what was the goodbye like?
- The first time rounds came in
- The longest day
- The funniest moment (there's always one)
- The soldier you think about most
- The decision that still wakes you up
- What you saw that you've never told anyone
- The day you came home — each time
- Did home feel like home when you got back?

### Part Four: The Mask
The 20 years of performing normalcy. PTSD unnamed. The cost of carrying it alone.

**Key questions to explore:**
- When did you first know something was wrong?
- What did "fine" look like from the inside?
- The moment you came closest to breaking
- Who almost saw through it?
- What was the 1SG mask made of — what did you tell yourself to keep going?
- The cost to your body (sleep, health, substances)
- The cost to your relationships
- When did you start building systems — was it before or after you knew why?
- The day you finally said it out loud

### Part Five: Lindsey
The love story. How you met. What she means. What she's endured.

**Key questions to explore:**
- How did you meet?
- What did you see in her that no one else saw?
- What does she see in you that you can't see yourself?
- The proposal
- The wedding
- The hardest year of the marriage
- How has she handled the PTSD?
- What do you wish you'd told her sooner?
- What does she make possible that you couldn't do alone?

### Part Six: Father
Three children. Three different humans. The man he is in their eyes.

**Key questions to explore:**
- The birth of each child — what did you feel?
- Rylan — what do you see of yourself in him?
- Emory — what do you see of yourself in her?
- Harlan — what do you hope he never has to learn the hard way?
- Your best day as a father
- Your worst day as a father
- What do you want them to know about you that they're too young to understand now?
- What did your father teach you about being a father? (By example or by absence)

### Part Seven: The Reckoning
The VA claim. The diagnosis. The rebuilding.

**Key questions to explore:**
- The day you filed
- The C&P exam — what was it like to say it all out loud to a stranger?
- The letter — 100% P&T. What did you feel?
- Permission or verdict?
- What changed after?
- What hasn't changed yet?
- The health protocol — fighting for the body after years of neglect
- Naltrexone — the decision to address alcohol honestly
- TRT — reclaiming the physical self

### Part Eight: The System
Life OS. The architecture of a man who turned operational discipline inward.

**Key questions to explore:**
- When did you start building this?
- What were you really building — a productivity system or a survival system?
- What does it mean that you built a Guardian Protocol to watch for your own deception?
- What's the relationship between the systems you built for soldiers and the system you built for yourself?
- Is the system the legacy, or is it the tool that makes the legacy possible?

### Epilogue: The Mantled Flame
What it all means. What he wants remembered. The letter to his children.

**Key questions to explore:**
- What is the Mantled Flame? Where did that come from?
- If you could only tell your children one thing about who you were, what would it be?
- What do you want them to do with this story?
- Are you proud of the man you've become?
- What's left to do?

## The Cinematic Vision

When enough material exists, the final product becomes:
1. **The Written Memoir** — Beautifully typeset, chapter-by-chapter, first person
2. **The Narration** — Tory records himself reading key passages (guided by the system)
3. **The Score** — Period-appropriate music mapped to each chapter (songs that meant something at the time)
4. **The Visuals** — Photographs, documents, locations, mapped to the narrative
5. **The Film** — A cinematic experience combining narration + music + visuals + text overlays

The system will prompt for photos, songs, and recordings at the appropriate time.
