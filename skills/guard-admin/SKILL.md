## Retirement Points Tracking

### Good Year Requirements
- Minimum 50 retirement points per anniversary year
- Anniversary year runs from [enlistment anniversary date — verify]
- Membership points: 15/year (automatic for being in the Guard)
- Drill points: 1 point per 4-hour period (typically 4 points per drill weekend = 48/year for 12 drills)
- Annual Training: 1 point per day (typically 15 points for 2-week AT)
- Correspondence/online courses: varies

### Point Audit Protocol
- Request annual points statement from unit admin (RMO)
- Compare against personal records
- Flag any missing drills or AT credits
- Ensure all correspondence courses are properly credited
- **Every uncredited point is money lost at retirement**

## RPED Calculation & Verification

### What RPED Means
- Retired Pay Eligibility Date — the day Tory can start collecting Guard retirement pay
- For non-regular (Guard/Reserve) retirement: age 60 minus early retirement credit
- Early retirement credit: 90 days of qualifying service reduces age requirement by 3 months
- Tory's RPED of 2040/09/07 (age 58) implies approximately 2 years of early credit

### Verification Steps
1. Request official RPED letter from HRC/NGB
2. Cross-reference with NGB 23A years of service
3. Verify early retirement credit calculation
4. Confirm no service breaks that would affect RPED
5. **Do this annually — RPEDs can change if records are corrected**

## Retired Pay Calculation

```
Formula: Base Pay x Multiplier

Multiplier = Total Retirement Points / 360

Base Pay = Pay grade (E-7) at time of retirement, using pay tables
           in effect when payments BEGIN (age 58), not when service ends

Estimated:
  Assume 4,000+ total points at retirement
  4,000 / 360 = 11.11 multiplier (this is a percentage of base pay)
  ...actually: Multiplier = Points / 360 = percentage
  E-7 base pay (2040 estimate, with raises): ~$4,800/mo
  4,000 points / 360 = 11.11 → wait, this formula gives the years equivalent

Correct formula:
  Monthly Pay = (Years of Service Equivalent x 2.5%) x Base Pay
  Years equivalent = Total Points / 360
  If 5,400 points: 5,400/360 = 15 years equivalent
  15 x 2.5% = 37.5%
  37.5% x base pay = retired pay

  Using $5,322.07 estimate from financial plan (verify against official estimate)
```

**Action:** Request an official retirement pay estimate from the Guard. The $5,322.07 figure should be verified.

## SBP Decision (Survivor Benefit Plan)

**This is a one-time, irrevocable decision at retirement enrollment.**

- SBP pays 55% of retired pay to surviving spouse
- Cost: ~6.5% of retired pay (pre-tax deduction)
- For $5,322/mo retired pay: cost ~$346/mo, benefit to Lindsey ~$2,927/mo
- Alternative: Use savings from not electing SBP to buy term life insurance
- **Analysis needed:** Compare SBP cost vs equivalent term life coverage
- Factor: Lindsey also gets VA DIC ($1,612/mo) if Tory's death is service-connected

**Flag for estate-guard:** SBP election is an estate planning decision.
