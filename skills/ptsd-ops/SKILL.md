# PTSD Operations — Medical (Mental Health Division)

**Mission:** Maintain operational readiness of the most critical system — the brain. PTSD is not a weakness. It's a service-connected condition that requires active management, just like any other medical condition. This skill tracks treatment, identifies patterns, and ensures Tory is actually getting the support he needs — not just saying he is.

---

## Why This Exists Separately

PTSD doesn't fit neatly into health-pull (which tracks macros and body comp) or health-recommendations (which optimizes supplements and protocols). Mental health has its own rhythm:

- **It's invisible in data.** You can't see PTSD in a macro log or a step count.
- **It affects everything.** Sleep, eating, patience with kids, work performance, relationship quality.
- **It's easy to ignore.** The ISTJ tendency is to power through. The military training reinforces it.
- **It requires honesty.** Standing Order #1 matters most here.

## PTSD Management Framework

### The Three Pillars

**1. Professional Treatment**
- Therapy (CPT, PE, EMDR — whatever modality works)
- Medication management (if applicable)
- Regular appointments (not just crisis-driven)
- VA provides this at no cost — there is zero financial barrier

**2. Daily Maintenance**
- Sleep hygiene (consistent schedule, wind-down routine)
- Physical exercise (30+ min, most days — proven PTSD benefit)
- Social connection (not isolation)
- Mindfulness or grounding practices (even 5 min/day)
- Limiting alcohol (depressant, disrupts sleep, worsens symptoms)

**3. Environmental Design**
- Reduce unnecessary stressors (system-optimizer's job)
- Protect recovery time (calendar-intel blocks this)
- Family understands and supports (family-ops tracks this)
- Work boundaries maintained (work-ops ensures this)

## Warning Signs Checklist

The system should watch for these patterns across all data sources:

| Signal | Where to See It | Threshold |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Sleep degradation | Health exports | <6 hrs avg or >30 min variance |
| Exercise dropout | Health exports | 3+ days missed when pattern is daily |
| Social withdrawal | EOD close, family presence | Missing dinners, canceling plans |
| Appetite changes | Macro data | Significant deviation from baseline |
| Increased irritability | [self-report only] | Snapping at kids, Lindsey |
| Avoidance behavior | Calendar | Canceling therapy, avoiding triggers |
| Hypervigilance | [self-report only] | Difficulty relaxing at home |
| Concentration problems | Work performance | Missing deadlines, forgetting tasks |
| Emotional numbness | [self-report only] | Going through motions |
| Substance increase | [self-report only] | More than baseline alcohol use |

**If 3+ signals are present simultaneously → FLAG in COP as CCIR condition.**

## VA Mental Health Resources

### Available at No Cost (100% P&T)
- **Individual therapy** — CPT, PE, EMDR, or other evidence-based modalities
- **Group therapy** — Veteran-specific groups
- **Medication management** — Psychiatry appointments
- **Telehealth** — Video appointments from home
- **Vet Centers** — Community-based, combat-specific counseling (separate from VA)
- **Whole Health** — Yoga, meditation, acupuncture, tai chi through VA
- **PTSD coaching app** — VA's PTSD Coach app (free, evidence-based)
- **Couples counseling** — Available through VA for service-connected issues

### Emergency Resources
- **Veterans Crisis Line:** 988 (press 1) — 24/7
- **Crisis Text Line:** Text 838255
- **VA Emergency:** Go to nearest VA ER
- **Vet Center Walk-In:** No appointment needed

## The Uncomfortable Truth About PTSD Management

PTSD doesn't get better by ignoring it. The military taught Tory to be strong — which is useful in combat and useless in recovery. Strength here means:

- Showing up to therapy even when it's uncomfortable
- Telling the truth about how he's actually doing
- Accepting that medication isn't weakness
- Recognizing when the system is covering for avoidance
- Understanding that his kids are watching how he handles hard things

The system cannot force any of this. It can only:
1. Track the data that reveals the pattern
2. Ask the question nobody else will ask
3. Make the resources impossible to forget
4. Flag when the signals say things aren't fine

**"I'm fine" is not an assessment. It's a deflection. The system doesn't accept it.**

## The Standard

A First Sergeant who sent Soldiers to sick call but never went himself would be a hypocrite. The same principle applies here. The system that optimizes investments, tracks macros, and monitors networks but ignores mental health is operating with a critical blind spot.

PTSD is the most honest thing about Tory's military service. Managing it well is the most important thing the system does.
