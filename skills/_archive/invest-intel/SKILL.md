**Mission:** Apply operational environment analysis to capital markets. Read the battlefield — geopolitics, supply chains, macro variables, technology shifts — and translate it into investment positioning. Weekly intelligence briefings. Event-driven alerts. Thesis-first, ticker-second.

## Investment Philosophy: The Owens Doctrine

Tory doesn't chase momentum or read candlestick charts. He reads systems. The investment thesis flows from a military-trained mind that thinks in:

1. **Vertical Integration** — Companies that control the full stack create compounding advantages. Tesla isn't a car company — it's a data collection platform (vehicles), an energy company (Solar/Powerwall), a robotics company (Optimus), a satellite company (via Musk ecosystem), and eventually a neural interface company (Neuralink). Each layer feeds the others. Each layer generates data. Data improves AI. AI improves every layer. This is a flywheel, not a product.

2. **Real-World Data Moats** — AI is only as good as its training data. The companies physically touching the real world — driving on roads, plowing fields, imaging patients, routing power, moving packages — are building data moats that pure-software companies cannot replicate. The question isn't "who has the best model?" It's "who has the data the models need?"

3. **Picks and Shovels** — Every gold rush makes the shovel makers rich. AI scaling requires chips (NVIDIA, TSMC, ASML), power (grid infrastructure, nuclear, natural gas), cooling (liquid cooling companies), raw materials (copper, lithium, rare earths, silicon), and connectivity (fiber, satellites). These aren't sexy. They're essential.

4. **Operational Environment** — Geopolitics shapes markets more than earnings calls. Ukraine disrupts energy and grain. Taiwan tensions threaten chip supply. CHIPS Act redirects capital. Iran affects oil. China trade policy affects everything. Politics IS the macro.

   - Is each investment thesis strengthening or weakening?
   - Any thesis-breaking developments?
   - New thesis emerging from operational environment changes?

   - Price action vs thesis

## Quarterly Deep Dive Protocol

Every quarter (aligned with earnings season), run a comprehensive review:

1. **Portfolio Audit:** Current positions, cost basis, gain/loss, thesis alignment
2. **Thesis Stress Test:** For each thesis, what would break it? Is that happening?
3. **Watchlist Refresh:** Add names where thesis strengthened, remove where it broke
4. **Allocation Check:** Core vs concentrated vs speculative — rebalance if drifted
5. **Tax Considerations:** Coordinate with tax-intel on harvesting gains/losses
6. **401k/TSP Review:** Are allocations still aligned with macro view?

## The Standard

Most retail investors react. They buy what went up yesterday and sell what went down. Tory's military training gives him an edge: he reads the operational environment, identifies the forces shaping the battlefield, and positions before the crowd understands what's happening.

The Tesla thesis isn't "TSLA stock will go up." It's "a company building multi-layered data flywheels across vehicles, energy, robotics, and neural interfaces is creating something the market doesn't yet know how to value." That's systems thinking applied to capital allocation.

This skill doesn't predict prices. It maps the operational environment, evaluates how reality is tracking against thesis, and surfaces the signals that matter. The rest is discipline.

"In war, the commander who understands the terrain wins. In markets, the investor who understands the system wins."
